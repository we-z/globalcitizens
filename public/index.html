Visa Types We Support

Tourist and Business Visas (B1/B2)

Overview

The B visas are primarily for people looking to enter the US for tourism, or to conduct business meetings, or to receive medical treatment.

Are You a Founder?

Startup founders often use the B visa to enter the U.S. to fundraise or attend an accelerator program. During their stay, they often build up enough evidence and switch to an O1 visa to stay in the U.S. longer to build their startup.

Our Advantage

B visa approvals are becoming increasingly challenging, with denial rates rising each year. Our process evaluates your case just as a consular officer would, identifying issues before you enter the interview. If your likelihood of approval is low, we provide tailored guidance and personalized materials to strengthen your case. Additionally, our streamlined system allows you to complete all paperwork in just 30 minutes and file the same day.

  • Visa Duration: 6 months
  • Our Application Workload: <30 minutes
  • Our Approval Rates: 99%+

O-1A

Overview

The O-1A visa is for individuals with extraordinary ability in their field.

Are You A Founder?

It caters to entrepreneurs, researchers, scientists, and experts with a proven record of achievements.

Our Advantage

Applications are processed within 2 to 3 months with premium processing available.

  • Visa Duration: 3 years
  • Our Application Workload: <45 minutes
  • Our Approval Rates: 97%+

4 Weeks to File

Global Citizens Guarantee

Our Timeline

  1. Gather evidence of extraordinary ability
    Collect all supporting documents that demonstrate your achievements, such as awards, publications, patents, and media recognition.
  2. Prepare documentation
    Compile a comprehensive application with letters of recommendation from notable industry professionals endorsing your expertise.
  3. File petition
    Submit your O-1A petition, ensuring that all required forms, fees, and evidence of extraordinary ability are included.
  4. Schedule interview
    If an interview is required, book an appointment at the nearest US embassy and prepare thoroughly for any questions.
  5. Attend interview
    Meet with the consular officer, present your case confidently, and provide additional details about your qualifications if requested.
  6. Receive visa approval
    Upon approval, you will receive your O-1A visa, allowing you to travel and work in the US under the specified conditions.
